Aslan Hodges has been the Sports Director for WAPT in Jackson, Mississippi since 2011, but he had a late start to the game. As an undergraduate at Florida State University, he had little idea of what he wanted to do in life. What he did know was this: there was no experience that lived up to Saturday’s in the fall rooting on his Seminoles at Doak Campbell Stadium. There was one other thing he came to realize, around his junior year: the only ‘professors’ he enjoyed listening to and learning from, were the ones on his TV, setting the stage for away games and breaking down all the action.

 

Upon graduating with honors and a degree in Poly-Sci and Communications, Aslan made the natural progression to sales. About a year in, he decided the cube life wasn’t the life for him and he ordered a copy of the Broadcast News Handbook. After putting together a demo reel with what little material he had amassed, the waiting game began.  Eventually, an opportunity arose in the form of a news and sports reporter position for KXLF in Butte, Montana. A mere six months later, he was offered the sports director position at KBZK in neighboring Bozeman.

 

One of Aslan’s greatest memories as a member of the press was covering Florida State at Doak Campbell Stadium. His alma mater was taking on their bitter rival, the University of Miami. With one minute left in the 4th quarter, The U botched a field goal, handing FSU a win and snapping its six-game losing streak to their rivals down south.
